# Unit 6 Reflection

[Reflect on your journey here]
In this unit, I learned about the testing phase and how to add links to my web page. Before this, my page was just one page with no way to go to other sites. Now I can add absolute links to other websites.
The most useful thing I learned was how to style links with CSS. I used :link, :hover, :active, and :visited to change the color of links when users interact with them. I think the hover effect is good because it tells users the link is clickable.
I also learned about favicons. I did not know the small icon in the browser tab had a special name. Adding a favicon makes the website look more professional and helps users find the page when they have many tabs open.
For polishing, I added a border to Kai's photo with border-radius to make the corners round. I also used the <span> tag to make the words "blue" and "green" show in their own colors. This makes the page more interesting.
One thing I found a bit difficult was getting feedback from other users. I asked three classmates to look at my page. They said the text was easy to read but one person suggested I make the headings bigger. I tried it but decided to keep my original size because I think it looks better.
Overall, this unit taught me that designing a website is not just about building it, but also about testing and improving it based on feedback.
